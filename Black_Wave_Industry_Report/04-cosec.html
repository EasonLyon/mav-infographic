Executive Summary

The Company Secretary industry in Malaysia is a vital component of the corporate governance framework, mandated by the Companies Act 2016. Every company in Malaysia must appoint a qualified company secretary.

The combined corporate secretarial services market (Singapore, Malaysia, China) was valued at USD 1,846.94 million in 2023 and is projected to grow to USD 3,244.41 million by 2032, at a CAGR of 6.5%. Key drivers include regulatory complexities, rising startups, and globalization demanding robust governance.

The role has evolved from administrative to strategic advisory, encompassing legal compliance, corporate governance, and ESG guidance. Growth opportunities lie in specialized advisory, technology leverage, and catering to foreign investments.

Industry Overview

A. Industry Definition

The Company Secretary industry in Malaysia comprises professionals and firms providing services mandated by the Companies Act 2016 and regulated by Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM). A company secretary ensures companies comply with legal requirements, advises on governance, maintains records, and facilitates communication.

Key Classifications:

NAICS Codes (Equivalents): 541110 (Lawyers), 541219 (Accounting), 541611 (Consulting)

SIC Codes (Equivalents): 8111 (Legal), 8721 (Accounting), 8742 (Consulting)

Regulatory & Policy Environment

A. Key National Regulations

  • Companies Act 2016 (CA 2016): Mandates CoSec appointment, duties.
  • SSM Act 2001: Regulates companies, businesses, CoSec licensing.
  • Malaysian Code on Corporate Governance (MCCG): Principles for PLCs.
  • Bursa Malaysia Listing Requirements: For PLCs.
  • AMLA: Due diligence, reporting obligations.
  • PDPA: Data protection compliance.

B. Impact of Compliance Requirements

  • Creates consistent demand for CoSec services.
  • Core function revolves around statutory adherence.
  • Crucial advisory role to avoid penalties.
  • Drives need for continuous learning & specialization (e.g., ESG).
  • Highlights critical nature of role due to risk of penalties.
  • CoSecs act as gatekeepers between company and regulators.

C. Taxation/Labor/Environmental Implications

  • Taxation: Ensure financial statement filings; maintain officer/shareholder records.
  • Labor: Advise on governance for employment policies; maintain director records.
  • Environmental (ESG): Advise on ESG disclosure; assist sustainability reporting.
